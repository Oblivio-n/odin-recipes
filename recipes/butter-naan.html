Butter Naan

Description

Naan is a leavened flatbread mostly cooked in a tandoor (clay oven). It is one of the most ordered flatbreads in Indian restaurants and is eaten with a curry or dal (lentils). Restaurants typically server many kinds of naans with varying thickess, textures and flavors.

Ingredients (1 cup= 240 mL)

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour (organic maida or 1 cup wheat flour + 1 cup all-purpose flour)
  • 1/2 cup plain yogurt (curd)
  • 6 to 8 tablespoons luke warm milk (1/4 cup + 2 tbsps) (may need more)
  • 2 tablespoons oil or melted butter
  • 2 teaspoons sugar
  • 1/2 to 3/4 teaspoon salt (adjust to taste)
  • 1 to 1.5 teaspoon grated garlic (optional)

    • For Leavening (or Use 3/4 Tsp Instant Yeast)

  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking soda

    • For toppings

  • 5 to 6 garlic cloves sliced or chopped
  • 3 tsp melted butter
  • 1/4 cup coriander leaves chopped finely
  • 1 tsp kalonji seeds (optional)

Steps

    Make dough

  1. To a mixing bowl add flour, baking powder, baking soda, salt, sugar and grated garlic.
  2. Pour yogurt, oil and 4 tps luke warm milk. Begin to mix to form a smooth dough adding more warm milk as needed. You may need up to half cup milk.
  3. Knead well until the dough turns soft, elastic and pliable. Wehen you press down the dough with your finger, it should dent easily.
  4. Optionally you can cover and rest the dough for 1 to 2 hours in a warm place.
  5. Grease your fingers with ghee or oil and divide the dough to 6 to 8 parts depending on the size you prefer.
  6. Make smooth balls and keep them covered until you finish rolling the naans.

    7. How to make Naan

  7. Sprinkle little flour on the rolling area and place a dough ball.
  8. Roll the ball to 8 to 9 inches long and 5 to 6 inches wide depending on the size the dough ball.
  9. Sprinkle kalonji seeds, sliced garlic and coriander leaves. Press down or roll gently.
  10. When you are done with 5 to 6 naans, begin to heat a griddle or pan.
  11. Ensure the pan is hot enough, then transfer the naan to it. Within a minute or so bubbles will be visible on top.
  12. With the help of a tong, flip it and cook either on the pan or diretly over the gas fire or stove. Or place a wired rack on the stove and toast it.
  13. When you see brown spots on the naan, remove them to a plate and brush with some butter. Ensure it is cooked through on both the sides well.
  14. Smear some butter on top and serve naan right away with kadhai paneer or any other curry.
