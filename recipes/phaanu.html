Phaanu

Description

The Phaanu is a traditional dish of Uttrakhand, a state in Northern India. Phaanu is a dish made up of horse gram lentils, which are locally grown in the hills. This delicious curry is cooked with several spices and herbs. It is generally served with hot pipping rice. The lentils are soaked in water for about 4 to 6 hours before its use. A different variety of lentils like the horse gram lentil, the pigeon pea lentil and the mung bean lentil can be used to prepare the Phanu.

Ingredients (1 cup = 240 mL)

  • Horse Gram lentil- 1 cup
  • Oil- 1/2 cup (preferably mustard oil)
  • Garlic- 4 to 5 cloves
  • Ginger- 1/2 inch piece
  • Green Chillies- 3 to 4
  • Jakhiya or Cumin Seeds- 1 tsp
  • Asfoetida- a pinch
  • Dry Coridander powder- 1/2 tsp
  • Turmeric powder- 1/4 tsp
  • Water- 3 cups
  • Salt according to taste

Steps

  1. Soak the Horse Gram Lentil in water overnight. If using Pea Pigeon Lentil, soak for 1-2 hrs.
  2. In the morning wash and rub the daal in running water so that it is free of seed covering. Then, grind it into a thick, dry paste in a mixer or on a silbatta along with green chillies, garlic and ginger.
  3. Place a tawa on a moderate flame, Put some oil and make thick pancakes (like cutlets) lentil paste. Use only half of the paste for making the cakes.
  4. Mix water with the remaining paste making it of pouring consistency. Heat oil in a pan and add jakhiya seeds and heeng. Now add Horse Gram Lentil paste, turmeric powder, dry coriander powder and salt.
  5. Cover and cook for about 10 mins on slow fire. Add the Lentil cakes to the gravy and continue to simmer for another 10 minutes. The gravy should have pouring consistency. If thick addd some more water and heat till it boils.
  6. Garnish the pure ghee and chopped coriander leaves. Serve with steamed rice.
